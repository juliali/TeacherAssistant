A great deal of life involves making reasonably quick and reasonably precise decisions with data. Sometimes these are decisions to which other people need answers ("how many of these posters should I print?"), and at other times they are spot-checks of claims of others ("I think we should print N posters"). 
You can't do this without basic arithmetic. You can do it with a basic knowledge of facts and basic arithmetic. And life becomes more manageable with this ability.
Multiplication tables are our entry into understanding quantity and measure, which are very basic life skills.
In short, these are requirements for being an informed and, in the best sense of the word, skeptical citizen.
